Why It Matters

Big Horn is a close-knit Wyoming community that values youth, health, and connection. However, the community faces a growing challenge:

  • No local indoor recreation facility, impacting youth through senior citizens.
  • Limited space for Early Childhood programs, which continue to grow.
  • Harsh climate conditions many months out of the year limit utilization of outdoor recreation facilities.
  • Lack of a central hub for connection and engagement - outside of our schools.

The Vision

This is more than just a building – it’s a catalyst for health, education, and unity. By combining recreation and education, we will:

  • Enhance quality of life for all ages.
  • Support families with state-of-the-art early childhood education spaces.
  • Give youth a place to be active, build confidence, and develop life skills.
  • Leave a lasting legacy for generations to come.

Early Learning Center

Our Early Learning Center will provide a safe, nurturing environment for children to grow and thrive. The center will focus on early childhood education, providing children with the skills and foundation needed to succeed in school and beyond. Key features of the center include:

  • Over 4000 sq ft of purpose built early learning space.
  • State-of-the-art classrooms designed for early childhood education.
  • Interactive learning areas that encourage creativity and exploration.
  • Safe and engaging play areas to foster physical development.
  • Trained educators dedicated to meeting the developmental needs of young children.
"These classrooms will shape the lives of our youngest residents for generations to come."

— Kendra Barney, Early Childhood Coordinator

Frequently Asked Questions

1. What is the Big Horn Community Recreation & Early Learning Center?

The Big Horn Community Recreation & Early Learning Center is a dual-purpose facility aimed at improving the health, education, and recreational needs of our community. It will serve as a hub for physical activities, sports, and early childhood education.

2. When will the center be built and open for use?

Upon hitting a predefined funding goal, we will begin the process of breaking ground.

3. Who will have access to the recreation center and early learning spaces?

The facility will be open to Big Horn K-12 and the entire community, with specific spaces dedicated to early childhood education programs through Sheridan County School District #1. Families and individuals of all ages will be able to enjoy sports, fitness programs, and recreational activities.

4. How can I support the development of the center?

You can support the development by donating to our fundraising campaign. We also encourage local businesses, organizations, and individuals to get involved by sponsoring programs, events, or contributing in-kind services.

5. How is this being funded?

At this point, entirely through private donations.

6. Who is leading this effort?

The Big Horn Baseball Club - a non-profit 501(c)(3) - is leading this effort. Having accomplished the development and construction of the baseball and little guy football fields at Big Horn, this is our next endeavor! If you would like to reach out to a member you can contact Jay Martinson, Travis Todd, Kendra Barney, or Nick Zent.

7. Who will own the Recreation Center?

Upon completion of construction, the facility will be owned, operated, and maintained by Sheridan County Recreation District #1.
